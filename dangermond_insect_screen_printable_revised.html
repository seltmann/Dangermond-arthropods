Ammopelmatus muwu
Point Conception Jerusalem cricket
Orthoptera | Stenopelmatidae
Habitat
Coastal sandy soils, dunes, sandy scrub, and loose terrace soils near Point Conception.
Host/resources
Omnivorous/predatory; likely roots, decaying vegetation, and small arthropods.
CA status
CDFW Special Animals List; G1/S1; IUCN Vulnerable.
Adult phenology
Mostly nocturnal; most likely surface-active during cool/wet periods and after rains.
Likely at Dangermond?
Yes - high priority.
Most likely observed
Point Conception coastal terrace, sandy scrub, dunes, under cover, pitfall traps, night road/track surveys.

Trimerotropis occulens
Lompoc grasshopper
Orthoptera | Acrididae
Habitat
Open sandy or sparsely vegetated coastal terrace grassland, scrub/grassland openings, and road cuts.
Host/resources
Herbivorous grasshopper; likely grasses and forbs.
CA status
CDFW Special Animals List; G1G2/S1S2; IUCN Endangered.
Adult phenology
Late spring-summer; records in June-August.
Likely at Dangermond?
Yes - high regional priority.
Most likely observed
Open terrace grasslands, sandy openings, road cuts, Burton Mesa/Lompoc/Vandenberg-type habitats.
iNaturalist taxon page
Coelus globosus
Globose dune beetle
Coleoptera | Tenebrionidae
Habitat
Coastal dunes, foredunes, backdunes, loose sand with native dune vegetation.
Host/resources
Detritivore/scavenger; associated with dune wrack, loose sand, and dune vegetation.
CA status
CDFW Special Animals List; G1G2/S1S2; IUCN Vulnerable.
Adult phenology
Detectable much of year by targeted search; often nocturnal or under sand/debris.
Likely at Dangermond?
Yes - high priority where dunes occur.
Most likely observed
Coastal dunes, wrack line, under driftwood/debris, dune vegetation margins.

Cicindela hirticollis gravida
Sandy beach tiger beetle
Coleoptera | Carabidae
Habitat
Moist sand near ocean beaches, swales behind dunes, and upper beach beyond normal high tide.
Host/resources
Predatory beetle; larvae in moist sand burrows.
CA status
CDFW tracks C. hirticollis gravida as a special-status sandy beach tiger beetle.
Adult phenology
Spring/fall activity likely; active on warm sunny days when sand is moist.
Likely at Dangermond?
Possible.
Most likely observed
Moist upper beach, creek-mouth sand, dune swales, protected beach strand.

Cicindela senilis frosti
Senile tiger beetle
Coleoptera | Carabidae
Habitat
Coastal saltmarsh, tidal mudflats, saline flats.
Host/resources
Predatory beetle; larvae in mud/sand burrows.
CA status
CDFW tracks C. senilis frosti as special-status.
Adult phenology
Approx. Feb-Jun and Aug-Oct for species-level activity.

Possible if saltmarsh/tidal-flat habitat is present.

Saltmarsh openings, unvegetated mudflats, saline flats, estuary margins.
iNaturalist taxon page
Bombus crotchii
Crotch’s bumble bee
Hymenoptera | Apidae
Habitat
Coastal scrub, grassland, chaparral openings, and restoration areas with sustained bloom.
Host/resources
Generalist floral resources; Fabaceae, Asteraceae, Lamiaceae, Boraginaceae, Asclepias, Salvia, Acmispon, Eriogonum, etc.
CA status
CDFW Special Animals List; G2/S2; CESA Candidate Endangered; IUCN Endangered.
Adult phenology
Queens late winter-spring; workers/males spring-late summer; best survey during abundant bloom.
Likely at Dangermond?
Yes - high priority.
Most likely observed
Flower-rich coastal scrub/grassland, vetch/deerweed/sage/buckwheat/milkweed patches, restoration plantings.

Bombus caliginosus
Obscure bumble bee / fog-belt bumble bee
Hymenoptera | Apidae
Habitat
Coastal grasslands, scrub, meadows, edge habitats; more coastal/fog-belt associated.
Host/resources
Generalist floral resources; requires nesting/overwintering habitat and sustained bloom.
CA status
CDFW Special Animals List; G2G3/S1S2; IUCN Vulnerable.
Adult phenology
Spring-fall; survey during peak coastal bloom.
Likely at Dangermond?
Possible, but ID is difficult.
Most likely observed
Flower-rich coastal grassland/scrub, moist/foggy coastal sites; voucher-quality photos or specimens needed.

Pseudocopaeodes eunus
Alkali skipper / wandering skipper complex
Lepidoptera | Hesperiidae
Habitat
Alkali meadow, saltgrass flat, saline wetland, saltmarsh transition.
Host/resources
Larval host: saltgrass, Distichlis spicata.
CA status
CDFW tracks P. e. eunus and P. e. obscurus; federal endangered status applies to Carson wandering skipper.
Adult phenology
Several flights Apr-Sep; adults nectar at flowers.
Likely at Dangermond?
Habitat-dependent.
Most likely observed
Saltgrass-dominated flats, saline meadow, saltmarsh transition zones.
